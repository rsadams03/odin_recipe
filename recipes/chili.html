Dank Ass Chili

Why this is dank.

Homemade chili paste. Homemade beef stock. Beef. Beans!

No commie Texans involved with this chili. We're not making a glizzy condiment afterall.

Ingredients

Chili Paste

  • 2 pasilla chiles (dried whole)
  • 4 arbol chiles (dried whole)
  • 4 chipotle chiles (dried whole)
  • 3 guajillo chiles (dried whole)
  • 3 ancho chiles (dried whole)
  • water

Main Shizz

  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • Olive oil
  • 1 white onion, roughly chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 4-6 cups beef stock
  • 1 (28oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 1 (14.5oz) can black beans
  • 1 (14.5oz) can kidney beans
  • 1 (14.5oz) can cannellini beans
  • Cinnamon
  • Ground allspice
  • Ground coriander
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried thyme
  • 2-4 bay leaves
  • 2-3 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1-2 limes, juiced
  • Shredded cheese, sour cream, etc for garnish

STEPS

  1. Relieve the dried chilis of their stems and seeds.
  2. Dry roast chilis in a pan to wake up their aroma.
  3. Once aromatic, add enough water to cover the chilis and bring to a simmer. Cover with a lid, kill the heat and let steep for around 10 minutes.
  4. Add chilis and steeping liquid to a blender and blend until smooth.
  5. Crank a pot up to medium high heat, add some olive oil and once hot, add the ground beef to begin browning.
  6. Once the meat is browned and a nice fond is on the bottom of the pot, add the onions and deglaze the pan.
  7. When onions are translucent and the fond is gone, add some tomato paste and cook until the raw tomato flavor disappears (around 1-3 minutes).
  8. Add the garlic and spices, then saut√© until fragrant.
  9. Add in the crushed tomatoes, beef stock, beans and chili paste. Bring to a simmer and continue simmering until the chili thickens and the flavors meld.
  10. Squeeze in some lime juice to finish and garnish with whatever your heart desires. I personally dig some cheese and sour cream.
